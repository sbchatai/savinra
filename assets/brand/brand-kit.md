## SECTION 1: BRAND STRATEGY

### 1.1 Mission
> To make sustainable, thoughtfully crafted Indo-Western fashion accessible to the modern Indian woman — proving that style and responsibility are not a compromise.

### 1.2 Vision
> To become the most trusted name in sustainable Indo-Western fashion in India — the label conscious women reach for when they want to look refined without abandoning their roots or the planet.

### 1.3 Core Values

| Value | What It Means for Savinra |
|-------|--------------------------|
| Refined sustainability | We use sustainable materials not as a marketing gimmick but as a non-negotiable standard. Every fabric choice has a reason. |
| Indo-Western authenticity | We don't dilute Indian craft for Western appeal or add Indian motifs as an afterthought. We create genuine fusion — garments that belong in both worlds. |
| Quiet confidence | We don't shout. Our clothes speak through cut, fabric, and finish — not logos, embellishments, or trend-chasing. |
| Thoughtful living | We believe in buying fewer, better things. Our customers choose us because they're intentional about what they wear and why. |

### 1.4 Positioning Statement
> For urban Indian women (25–40) who want to dress with intention, **Savinra** is the Indo-Western fashion label that blends sustainable materials with refined silhouettes — because modern Indian style shouldn't mean choosing between heritage and the future.

### 1.5 Brand Archetype

**Primary: The Ruler** — Refined, authoritative, premium. Savinra sets a standard, not follows one. The gold palette, serif typography, and "House of Refined Living" tagline all express this. The Ruler archetype manifests as quiet authority — not ostentatious luxury, but the calm assurance of someone who knows exactly what they want.

**Secondary: The Creator** — Inventive, expressive, craft-driven. The Indo-Western fusion angle is inherently creative — taking two traditions and creating something new. The sustainability focus adds intentionality to the making process. This secondary archetype shows up in how Savinra talks about its process, materials, and design philosophy.

### 1.6 Competitive Positioning

Savinra occupies a distinctive space: **premium but not couture, sustainable but not earthy, Indian but not traditional.**

| Axis | Savinra's Position | vs. Typical Competitors |
|------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Price | Mid-to-premium | Fast fashion is cheaper; designer labels are pricier |
| Aesthetic | Refined minimalism | Others lean either heavily ethnic or heavily western |
| Sustainability | Core identity | Most boutiques treat this as optional or marketing |
| Audience | Intentional urban women | Most target either mass or ultra-luxury |

Key competitors to watch: FabIndia (accessible but lacks fusion edge), Nicobar (similar vibe but more home/lifestyle), Anita Dongre Grassroot (sustainable but fully ethnic). Savinra differentiates by being purely Indo-Western fusion, sustainably made, at boutique-accessible pricing.

### 2.3 Color Palette

| Role | Name | HEX | Usage |
|------|------|-----|-------|
| Primary | Savinra Gold | #D4AF37 | Logo, decorative elements, large display text, dark-bg buttons, badges, accents |
| Primary (accessible) | Antique Bronze | #8C7A2E | CTA buttons on light bg, gold text on light bg, links, headers when gold is too light |
| Secondary | Leaf Sage | #9FAF90 | Sustainability badges, secondary sections, collection banners, leaf motif |
| Neutral warm | Ivory Linen | #F5ECDA | Card backgrounds, hero section overlays, highlighted areas |
| Neutral light | Parchment | #FAF8F3 | Page background — the default canvas |
| Neutral dark | Deep Cocoa | #2C2622 | Body text, headings, footer backgrounds, dark UI elements |

**Extended palette (status/functional):**

| Role | HEX | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| Success | #5A8A6A | Order confirmed, payment success, in-stock |
| Warning | #C49B2A | Low stock, pending actions |
| Error | #B5453A | Payment failed, out of stock, errors |
| Info | #6B8DA6 | Informational messages, tracking updates |

**Color Rationale:**
- **Savinra Gold (#D4AF37):** Gold carries strong aspirational and prosperity associations in India. It signals premium without being cold (unlike black luxury brands). Directly from the logo — the brand's signature. The golden metallic shine in physical applications (business cards, packaging) creates a tactile luxury cue.
- **Antique Bronze (#8C7A2E):** The accessible workhorse. Darker gold that passes WCAG AA on light backgrounds (5.1:1 contrast). Used for all interactive elements where gold would be illegible. Feels like a patina — aged, rich, intentional.
- **Leaf Sage (#9FAF90):** Extracted from the leaf motif in the logo. Connects to sustainability without the cliché "eco green." Muted, sophisticated, pairs naturally with gold. In Indian color context, sage avoids the religious associations of brighter greens.
- **Ivory Linen (#F5ECDA):** Warmth without yellow. Evokes natural fabrics — linen, raw silk, handloom. Used for card backgrounds to create depth against the page background.
- **Parchment (#FAF8F3):** Barely-there warm white. The page breathes. Never use pure white (#FFFFFF) — it's too clinical for this brand.
- **Deep Cocoa (#2C2622):** Warm near-black. Body text that feels softer than pure black but maintains excellent readability. Has a brown undertone that aligns with the natural/earthy brand DNA.

**Accessibility Notes:**
- Deep Cocoa on Parchment: 14.2:1 — Passes WCAG AAA
- Antique Bronze on Parchment: 5.1:1 — Passes WCAG AA
- Savinra Gold on Parchment: 2.8:1 — FAILS. Gold is decorative only on light bg, never for body text
- White on Deep Cocoa: 13.8:1 — Passes WCAG AAA (for dark sections)
- White on Savinra Gold: 2.3:1 — Large text/decorative only (16px+ bold or 19px+ regular)

**The Golden Shine — Core Brand Identity Element:**

The metallic golden shine is NOT a decorative accent — it is the brand's visual signature. Every instance of the brand name "SAVINRA" and the tagline "House of Refined Living" must carry the golden shine effect. This is the single most recognizable element of the brand identity.

**Where the golden shine MUST appear:**
- Logo/brand name — everywhere it appears (header, footer, splash screen, about page, loading screen)
- Tagline "House of Refined Living" — always with the shine
- Hero section headlines that contain the brand name
- Section titles on brand-focused pages (About Us, Our Story)
- Loading/splash screen animation
- Order confirmation headers ("Thank you for choosing Savinra")
- Email/WhatsApp headers that feature the brand name
- Admin panel logo and brand name in sidebar
- App install prompt banner
- Favicon/PWA icon background accent
- Product tags or "Savinra Original" badges

**Where the golden shine must NOT appear:**
- Body text or product descriptions (readability issue)
- Navigation menu items (too distracting for repeated scanning)
- Form labels, input fields, or error messages
- Price text (needs to be instantly scannable)
- Small text below 14px (gradient becomes muddy)

**Golden Shine Gradient Stops (for any implementation):**
- Darkest gold: #8C7A2E (Antique Bronze)
- Core gold: #D4AF37 (Savinra Gold)
- Highlight: #F5E6A3 (Light Gold — the "shine" peak)

**Fallback rule:** In any context where CSS gradients on text are not supported (email clients, plain text, some social media), use flat #D4AF37 as the fallback color. The shine is the ideal; the flat gold is the acceptable minimum.

### 2.4 Typography

**Heading Font:** Cormorant Garamond — Weight 600 (Semi-Bold) and 700 (Bold)
> An elegant, high-contrast serif with roots in Garamond's classical letterforms. It feels literary, refined, and distinctly non-corporate. Used in the logo — extends naturally to all headings. Its tall x-height and sharp serifs read as confident and editorial.
> Source: Google Fonts — https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Cormorant+Garamond:wght@400;500;600;700&display=swap

**Body Font:** Lato — Weight 400 (Regular) and 500 (Medium)
> Clean, humanist sans-serif with warm curves. Provides excellent readability at small sizes while staying neutral enough to let Cormorant Garamond be the star. The slight warmth in its letterforms avoids the coldness of geometric sans-serifs.
> Source: Google Fonts — https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Lato:wght@300;400;500;700&display=swap

**Typography rules:**
- Maximum 2 fonts. Never introduce a third.
- Headings in Cormorant Garamond. Everything else in Lato.
- Product names always in Cormorant Garamond — they are mini-headlines.
- Prices in Cormorant Garamond with ₹ symbol — gives pricing a premium feel vs. using a sans-serif.
- Letter-spacing: +0.5px on Lato buttons and labels (uppercase or small text). Never letter-space Cormorant Garamond — it was designed for tight setting.
- Line height: 1.6 for body text, 1.2 for headings.

### 2.6 Visual Language

**Overall aesthetic:** Editorial, warm, natural, restrained

**Photography style:**
- Natural light, warm tones — never harsh studio lighting
- Linen/cotton/natural fabric textures as backgrounds
- Models in effortless, confident poses — never overly styled or rigid
- Earthy, muted backgrounds (terracotta walls, wooden textures, woven fabrics, sun-dappled courtyards)
- Indian settings without being touristy — think courtyard of an old haveli, not the Taj Mahal
- AI-generated model photos: use Indian models, studio/lifestyle backgrounds, consistent warm tone editing

**Graphic elements:**
- The leaf motif from the logo can be used as a subtle watermark or section divider — always in Leaf Sage at 15-20% opacity
- Thin gold lines (1px, #D4AF37) as section dividers — never thick borders
- Rounded corners at 8px on cards — premium but not overly soft
- Subtle shadows only (0 2px 8px rgba(44,38,34,0.08)) — never harsh drop shadows

**Spacing & density:**
- Airy. Generous whitespace. The brand needs to breathe.
- Minimum 24px padding inside cards, 16px between elements
- Hero sections: large, image-forward, minimal text overlay
- Product grids: never more than 3 columns on desktop, generous gaps (20px+)

**What to avoid visually:**
- Pure white backgrounds (#FFFFFF) — always use Parchment (#FAF8F3)
- Pure black text — always use Deep Cocoa (#2C2622)
- Busy patterns, complex illustrations, or heavy ornamentation
- Neon or bright accent colors — nothing should scream
- Stock photo aesthetic — no posed models on white backgrounds
- Gradients (except the logo shine effect described above)
- Heavy borders or box outlines — prefer shadows or background color differentiation

## SECTION 3: BRAND VOICE & MESSAGING

### 3.1 Brand Personality

Savinra speaks like: A 35-year-old woman who runs a design studio in Jaipur. She's well-travelled, deeply rooted in Indian craft traditions, and quietly confident about her taste. She doesn't explain why sustainable fashion matters — she assumes you already know. She talks about fabric, craft, and intention rather than trends, sales, and urgency. At a dinner party, she's the one people gravitate toward — not because she's loud, but because everything she says is considered and specific.

### 3.2 Tone of Voice Spectrum

| Attribute | Our Position |
|-----------|-------------|
| Warm ←————→ Authoritative | **70% warm, 30% authoritative.** We're inviting but never casual. We know our craft and it shows, but we lead with warmth. |
| Playful ←————→ Serious | **80% serious.** We're not playful — but we're never heavy. Think "considered" not "corporate." The occasional wry observation is fine. |
| Simple ←————→ Sophisticated | **70% sophisticated.** We use rich, specific language (fabric names, craft techniques) but never jargon for jargon's sake. Always elegant, never academic. |
| Bold ←————→ Understated | **85% understated.** This is core to our identity. We suggest rather than declare. We show rather than tell. No exclamation marks, no ALL CAPS in marketing, no "limited time only!!!". |

### 3.3 We Are / We Are Not

| We Are | We Are Not |
|--------|-----------|
| Refined | Stuffy or pretentious |
| Intentional | Preachy about sustainability |
| Confident | Arrogant or exclusionary |
| Warm | Overly familiar or chatty |
| Specific (we name fabrics, techniques, origins) | Vague or generic ("best quality," "premium materials") |
| Unhurried | Indifferent or slow to respond |

### 3.4 Messaging Framework

**Brand Promise:**
> Every Savinra piece is designed with intention — where Indian craft meets modern silhouettes, made from materials that respect the earth.

**Elevator Pitch:**
> Savinra is a sustainable Indo-Western fashion label for the modern Indian woman. We design fusion silhouettes — kurtas reimagined with western cuts, Indian textiles shaped into contemporary forms — using fabrics that are kind to the planet. For women who want to dress with both roots and purpose.

**Homepage Hero Headline:**
> Where heritage finds its future

**Sub-headline:**
> Sustainable Indo-Western fashion, crafted for the woman who dresses with intention.

**Tagline:**
> House of Refined Living

### 3.5 Messaging Pillars

| Pillar | What We Say | Example Message |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Craft & intention | Every garment has a story — from the weave to the cut. We talk about how things are made, not just how they look. | "Hand-loomed Chanderi silk, cut into a silhouette that moves from a morning meeting to an evening gathering." |
| Sustainable by default | We don't lecture about sustainability — we just do it. Materials speak for themselves. | "100% organic cotton. Naturally dyed. Because what touches your skin should be as considered as the design." |
| Modern Indian identity | We celebrate the woman who lives in both worlds — rooted and contemporary. Not choosing between Indian and Western — wearing both. | "For the woman who pairs a handloom kurta with tailored trousers. Who texts in Hindi and presents in English. Who doesn't see a contradiction." |

### 3.6 Language Do's and Don'ts

**Do use:**
- Specific fabric and craft language: "Chanderi silk," "hand-block printed," "organic khadi" — not "premium fabric"
- Sensory words: "drapes softly," "breathes in summer heat," "the weight of raw silk"
- Second person ("you", "your") — make it personal
- Short, considered sentences. Let whitespace do the work.
- Indian English naturally — "kurta," "dupatta," "lehenga" without italics or explanation. Our customer knows.

**Never use:**
- "Affordable luxury" — we don't position on price
- "Limited time offer!" / "Hurry!" / "Don't miss out!" — we never create false urgency
- "Best quality" / "Premium" as standalone claims — too vague, too generic
- Hashtag-heavy language or meme references — not our voice
- "Eco-friendly" — overused and vague. Be specific: "organic cotton," "natural dyes," "zero-waste cut"
- Exclamation marks in product descriptions or brand copy (okay sparingly in social)

## SECTION 4: CHANNEL GUIDELINES

### 4.1 Website (Store PWA)

**Hero Section:** Full-bleed photography with minimal text overlay. Headline in Cormorant Garamond, max 6 words. Sub-headline in Lato, one line. Single CTA button. The first impression should be visual — the clothes and the mood do the selling.

**Product Pages:** Lead with photography (large, swipeable gallery). Product name in Cormorant Garamond. Description should open with the fabric/material ("Organic cotton khadi, hand-spun in...") then silhouette details, then care. Price in Cormorant Garamond, prominent but not louder than the product name. Keep descriptions under 80 words.

**Collection Pages:** Each collection gets a name that evokes a mood or place, not a season. ("The Courtyard Collection" not "Spring 2026"). Brief intro paragraph (2-3 sentences max), then products.

**CTAs:** Always action verbs, never "Submit" or "Click here."
- "Shop the collection" (not "See all")
- "Add to bag" (not "Add to cart" — "bag" feels more fashion)
- "Complete your look" (for cross-sells)
- "Place order" (checkout)

**Footer:** Warm, not corporate. Include the tagline, sustainability commitment (one line), and contact. Parchment text on Deep Cocoa background.

### 4.2 Social Media

**Instagram (Primary platform)**
- Owns: Behind-the-craft content, fabric stories, styling inspiration, new arrivals
- Post style: Single product shots or carousels (3-5 images). Clean, warm-toned. Never busy or over-styled.
- Caption style: Open with a sensory or evocative line (never "Introducing our new..."). 3-5 lines max. End with a soft CTA ("Link in bio" or a question). Never use more than 5 hashtags.
- Caption opener rule: Never start with the brand name or "We." Start with the garment, the fabric, or the woman wearing it.
- Example: "Chanderi silk, reimagined as a structured blazer. Light enough for a Delhi summer. Considered enough for every room you walk into."
- Stories: Process shots (fabric selection, cutting, packaging), customer features, quick polls on new designs
- Reels: Keep minimal — slow, editorial-style product reveals with ambient music. No trending audio, no jump cuts, no pointing at text.
- What NOT to post: Memes, trend-jumping, aggressive sale posts, stock photos, inspirational quotes on colored backgrounds

**Facebook**
- Mirror Instagram content with slightly longer captions
- Use for event announcements, customer reviews, collection launches
- Less frequent than Instagram (3-4x/week vs daily)

### 4.3 WhatsApp Communication

**Order notifications:**
- Greeting: "Hello [Name]" — warm but not overly casual
- Tone: Clear, informative, with one warm touch per message
- Sign-off: "Team Savinra" (not just "Savinra" — adds human warmth)
- Example order confirmation: "Hello Priya, your order #SAV-1234 has been confirmed. Your Chanderi Silk Kurta Set will be carefully packed and ready for shipping within 2 days. Thank you for choosing Savinra. — Team Savinra"
- Never use emojis in transactional messages (they undercut the premium feel)
- Abandoned cart messages: Gentle, never pushy. "Hello [Name], you left something beautiful in your bag — your [product name] is still waiting. Shop when you're ready." One reminder max with a discount, never three aggressive follow-ups.

**Re-engagement:**
- Tone: Like a thoughtful note, not a sales blast
- "Hello [Name], it's been a while. We've added some new pieces to the studio that we think you'd love. Here's a first look, just for you."

### 4.4 AI Chatbot Voice

The chatbot should feel like a knowledgeable, friendly store assistant — not a bot. Guidelines:
- Use "I" not "we" — the chatbot is a singular helpful presence
- Know fabrics, materials, sizing — be genuinely useful
- If asked about sustainability: be specific, never preachy. "This kurta is made from organic cotton grown in Gujarat, naturally dyed with indigo. Happy to share more about our process."
- Never say "I'm an AI" unprompted. If asked directly, be honest: "I'm Savinra's digital concierge — happy to help you find what you're looking for."
- Escalation: "I'd love to connect you with our team for that. Want me to send them a WhatsApp?"

## SECTION 5: BRAND DO'S AND DON'TS

### Always Do
1. Lead with the garment's story (material, origin, craft), not its price or discount
2. Use generous whitespace — the brand needs breathing room in every touchpoint
3. Use Indian fashion vocabulary naturally (kurta, dupatta, pallu) — our customer doesn't need translations
4. Be specific about sustainability claims — name the fabric, the source, the technique
5. Use the approved color palette strictly — no improvised shades of gold or green

### Never Do
1. Never create urgency through scarcity tactics ("Only 3 left!", countdown timers, flash sales)
2. Never use pure black (#000000) or pure white (#FFFFFF) — always use Deep Cocoa and Parchment
3. Never lecture about sustainability — our customer is already aligned, she doesn't need convincing
4. Never use more than 2 fonts or introduce off-brand colors
5. Never use "luxury" as a descriptor — we are refined, not luxurious. Luxury is a price signal; refinement is a values signal.
6. Never use generic stock photography — every image should feel like it belongs in Savinra's world
